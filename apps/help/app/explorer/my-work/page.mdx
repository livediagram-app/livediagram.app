**My Work** is your own library: the [diagrams](/explorer/all) you have created, organised however suits you. It is the tree in the middle of the Explorer sidebar, holding a collapsible **Dynamic** parent for the automatic buckets and whatever folders you have made.

## The Dynamic buckets

At the top of My Work, a collapsible **Dynamic** parent groups three live views that file themselves, so your own folders lead the tree:

- **Unsorted** catches diagrams you have not filed anywhere, so a freshly created diagram always has a home without you deciding anything first.
- **Generated** collects AI-made diagrams you have not filed yet, keeping them apart from the ones you drew.
- **Offline** collects diagrams saved only in this browser ([Offline Mode](/help/privacy-and-security/offline-mode/)).

None of the buckets can be renamed or deleted, and each shows a count badge only when it holds something. Filing a diagram into a folder of your own moves it out of Unsorted or Generated; Offline is different, listing every browser-only diagram whatever folder it sits in, so there is always one place to find them.

## Folders

Folders give your library structure without forcing it. Every diagram is in exactly one folder, or none, and folders can nest, so you can build something like "Projects / Customer / Engagement" when you want that depth, or keep everything flat under Unsorted when you do not.

<Step n={1}>
  Create a folder with the **New folder** action (the plus beside the My Work heading adds one at
  the top level). Each folder's own menu offers **New subfolder** for deeper levels.
</Step>
<Step n={2}>
  Move a diagram with its **Move to folder** menu item, which opens a tree of destinations, or drag
  a diagram row straight onto a folder.
</Step>
<Step n={3}>Rename or delete a folder from its menu.</Step>

Deleting a folder is safe for its contents: the diagrams inside drop to Unsorted and any subfolders move up to the top level, so nothing is ever lost when you tidy up.

## Two places to browse

You will meet My Work in two forms: the **full-page Explorer**, modelled on a desktop file browser, and the compact **floating Explorer panel** in the editor for finding a diagram mid-task. Both work whether or not you are signed in: a guest sees the diagrams and folders their own browser owns, and can sign in later to carry that work into an account.

<Tip>
  Do not over-organise on day one. Start in Unsorted, and pull diagrams into folders once a real
  grouping emerges. Folders are easy to add and rearrange whenever you need them.
</Tip>

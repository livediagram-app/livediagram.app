**Unsorted** is the Explorer's catch-all. Any diagram that isn't filed in a [folder](/help/explorer/folders/) lives here, so nothing is ever lost just because you haven't organised it yet.

<Figure caption="Diagrams with no folder collect in Unsorted at the top of My Work.">

</Figure>

## What lands in Unsorted

- **New diagrams** you create without opening a folder first.
- **Diagrams freed from a deleted folder**: when you delete a folder, the diagrams that were directly inside it return to Unsorted rather than being deleted.

In short, a diagram is in Unsorted when it has no folder of its own, with two exceptions that get their own buckets: unfiled AI-generated diagrams collect in **Generated**, and diagrams saved only in this browser collect in **Offline**, so Unsorted holds just the unfiled work you made yourself.

## It's a view, not a real folder

Unsorted is a synthetic group the Explorer shows for convenience, not a folder you created. So it behaves a little differently from your own folders:

- You can't rename or delete it, and you can't nest folders inside it.
- Its count badge only shows when there's something in it, so an organised library doesn't add noise.
- It has its own section so you can always find unfiled work: expand the **Dynamic** parent at the top of My Work and pick **Unsorted**, or visit `/explorer/unsorted`.

## Tidying up

To clear Unsorted, move its diagrams into [folders](/help/explorer/folders/). Open a diagram row's menu, choose **Move to folder…**, and pick a destination. As soon as a diagram has a folder, it leaves Unsorted.

<Tip>
  Treat Unsorted like an inbox: let new diagrams collect there while you work, then file them into
  folders in a quick pass when you have a moment.
</Tip>

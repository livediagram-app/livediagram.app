A drop shadow lifts an element off the canvas: cards read as cards, hero shapes get depth, and a key element separates from a busy background. The **Shadow** controls live in the element's right-click menu, right after Border, and work on anything that draws a visible body — shapes (icons and data shapes included), sticky notes, images, and link cards. Text, arrows, and freehand strokes don't carry shadows (a rectangular shadow under a line reads as a glitch, not a style).

## Picking a preset

Right-click an element and open the **Shadow** category. Five tiles cover the common looks:

- **None** removes the shadow.
- **Soft**, **Drop**, and **Lifted** are increasing steps of elevation, from a whisper of depth to floating well off the page.
- **Hard** is the zero-blur offset shadow, the crisp "poster" look.

Hovering a tile previews it live on the element; clicking commits it. The same category appears in the multi-select toolbar's Style flyout, where a preset applies to the whole selection at once.

## Dialling it in with sliders

Below the presets, four sliders give you the exact shadow:

- **Offset X** and **Offset Y** (each up to 24px either way) set where the shadow falls, which reads as where the light comes from.
- **Blur** (0 to 48px) sets how soft the edge is; zero is a hard edge.
- **Opacity** sets the shadow's strength.

Dragging any slider while the element has no shadow starts you from the Drop preset rather than from nothing, so the first drag always produces a visible shadow you can then shape. A whole slider drag is one undo step.

## Good to know

- **The colour is fixed** to a dark ink, so shadows always read as shade and keep working when you switch [themes](/help/canvas/themes/). Themes never add or change shadows.
- **Exports match the canvas**: PNG, SVG, and PDF exports, plus [snapshots and embeds](/help/collaboration/sharing/live-image/), render the same shadow you see.
- **The [format painter](/help/tips-and-tricks/format-painter/) copies shadows** along with the rest of an element's styling. **Reset style** clears the shadow; **Reset to theme** leaves it alone, since it only touches colours.

<Tip>
  Shadows are strongest when they're scarce. Lift the one card that matters with **Drop** and leave
  its neighbours flat, rather than shadowing everything, and the eye goes exactly where you want.
</Tip>

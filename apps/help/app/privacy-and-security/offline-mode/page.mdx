Most diagrams are saved to livediagram's servers so you can reach them from any device and share them. **Offline Mode** is the opposite choice: a diagram saved **only in the browser you made it in**, that never touches the server at all. It suits private, local-first work, or simply wanting a diagram that physically never leaves your machine.

## Creating an offline diagram

Offline Mode is off by default and chosen per diagram, so you can keep normal diagrams and offline ones side by side.

- On the **New Diagram** screen, step through the wizard to the **Settings** step (the third step, after Template and Theme) and turn on **"Save Offline, This Browser Only"**, then click **Create**.
- The diagram is written to your browser's local storage and opens like any other. An **Offline** badge next to its name, and an **Offline** tag in your Explorer list, mark it so you always know which diagrams are local-only.

<Note>
  An offline diagram is **not synced and not backed up**. It lives only in this browser: clearing
  your site data, or the browser reclaiming storage, will delete it. Keep anything important on the
  cloud, or export a copy.
</Note>

## What's different

An offline diagram is a normal diagram: every editor feature works, including shapes, arrows, layers, tabs, themes, undo/redo, and export. Only the features that need the server are unavailable, because there's no server copy to power them:

- **Sharing** needs a diagram on the server, so opening **Share** on an offline diagram prompts you to sync it to your account first. Embeds are unavailable for the same reason.
- **Live collaboration** and **teams** don't apply; an offline diagram is yours alone.
- The AI assistant works while you're online (it reads your current canvas), but not with no connection.
- Images you add are stored **inside the diagram**, so it stays fully local.

## Moving to and from your account

You can change your mind at any time, from the diagram's menu in the Explorer.

- **Sync Diagram** uploads an offline diagram to the cloud. It becomes a normal diagram, shareable and reachable from your other devices, and the local-only copy is removed.
- **Take Offline** does the reverse: it downloads a cloud diagram into this browser and then **removes it from your account and every other device**. Because that deletes the server copy for good, we ask you to confirm first.

<Note>
  "Take Offline" is deliberate and irreversible on the server side: once a diagram is offline it
  exists only in the browser you took it offline in. If it was shared, taking it offline also
  revokes the share links.
</Note>

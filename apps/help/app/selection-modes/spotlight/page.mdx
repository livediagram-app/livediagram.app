The Spotlight mode dims the canvas except a soft circle of light that follows your cursor, so a viewer's attention lands on one spot at a time. Choose it from the mode picker at the top of the floating palette.

## How it works

As you move the cursor, the circle of light moves with it while everything outside stays dimmed. **Left-click grows the circle and right-click shrinks it**, so you can match the beam to a single shape or open it up over a whole cluster. This is useful when you are talking through a diagram and want to focus the room on one area before moving on to the next.

## Availability

Spotlight is **desktop only**, and is disabled on an empty canvas. Add an element on a desktop browser and the mode becomes available.

<Tip>
  Spotlight works well alongside the Laser Pointer when presenting: dim the rest of the canvas, then
  point out the detail you want to discuss.
</Tip>

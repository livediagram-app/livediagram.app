The **Icons** tab is a searchable catalogue of single-colour, line-art icons. Because they are drawn in one colour, every icon takes on your theme's colour automatically, so a whole diagram stays visually consistent.

## Browsing by category

The catalogue is organised into categories so you can browse when you are not sure what you want: **Animated**, **Tech**, **People**, **Security**, **Files**, **Charts**, **Arrows**, **Furniture**, **UI**, and **Emoji**. Open a category to see every icon it holds.

## Searching and placing

When you know what you need, search by name (for example "server", "heart", or "calendar") and the catalogue filters to matching icons as you type. Click one to place it on the canvas, then resize and position it like any other element. Its colour follows your theme, so changing the theme recolours every placed icon at once.

Type while an icon is selected to give it a **caption**. The glyph always keeps to the side opposite the text, move the caption to the top and the art drops below it, align it left and the art shifts right, so the two never overlap however you arrange them.

## Emoji

The **Emoji** category holds around 60 native colour emoji: smileys and reactions (thumbs up, party, thinking), status marks (check, warning, fire, rocket), and everyday work objects (calendar, pin, bug, memo). They are searchable like every other icon, and you place them the same way, click to add one to the canvas, or **drag one onto a shape** to sit beside its label as a status marker, exactly as any icon does.

Unlike the line-art icons, emoji are real colour glyphs, so they **keep their own colours whatever the theme**: a thumbs up stays yellow and a fire stays orange however you restyle the diagram.

## Line-art icons versus brand marks

These icons are deliberately single-colour and abstract. If you need full-colour brand marks, the official logos for cloud providers and infrastructure, look in the [Technology](/help/palette/technology/) tab instead, which keeps AWS, Azure, and similar icons in their official colours rather than your theme colour.

The palette's **Tools** tab gathers the elements that are not plain shapes. The tiles are grouped by what you are doing: **Write** for text and notes, **Draw** for the sketching tools, **Structure** for grids and containers, **Blocks** for rich content cards, **People &amp; media** for pictures and figures, and **Data** for charts and meters. Each group is a collapsible section: one is open at a time (Write, when you first open the tab), and clicking a heading switches to that group.

Not sure which group holds the tool you're after? A **search box** sits above the groups, just like the one on the Icons tab: type a name ("table", "chart", "pencil") and the groups give way to a single grid of every matching tool. Clear the search to return to the groups.

## The groups

Click a group's heading to open it; the previous group folds away so the tab stays compact.

**Write** holds the wordy elements: **Text** drops a free-standing label, **Sticky Note** is a coloured note card, and **Annotation** is a small note marker for callouts.

**Draw** holds the gesture tools: **Pencil** lets you sketch freehand, with an optional shape-recognition mode; **Highlighter** lays a wide translucent marker stroke for calling attention to things; **Polygon** places straight-edged outlines point by point; and **Arrow** drops a plain connector you can position by hand.

**Structure** is for organising: **Table** is an editable grid of cells, **Frame** is a labelled container you group work inside, and **Timeline Rail** is a horizontal track for sequencing events.

**Blocks** holds the rich content cards: **Code Block** is a dark monospace card for syntax-highlighted snippets, **Checklist** is a card of tickable to-do rows, and **Link Card** is a clickable preview of a URL.

**People &amp; media** covers pictures and figures: **Image** places an uploaded picture, **Avatar** places a circular image, and **User** places an actor figure, both handy for people in a flow.

Each behaves like any other element once placed: select, restyle, group, and move it freely.

## Guides in this tab

Several tools have their own walkthroughs, listed below and linked here:

- [Drawing and Sketch](/help/palette/tools/drawing/) covers the Pencil, Highlighter, and Polygon tools, plus shape recognition.
- [Images](/help/palette/tools/images/) covers uploading and placing pictures, avatars, and user figures.
- [Tables](/help/palette/tools/tables/) covers the editable grid: adding one, filling cells, and rows/columns.
- [Sticky Notes](/help/palette/tools/sticky-notes/) covers the coloured note card for short annotations.
- [Code Blocks](/help/palette/tools/code-blocks/) covers the dark monospace card for syntax-highlighted snippets.
- [Checklists](/help/palette/tools/checklists/) covers the card of checkable to-do rows you tick on the canvas.
- [Data and Chart Elements](/help/palette/tools/data-elements/) covers the Data sub-section: Pie Chart, Bar Chart, Line Chart, Progress Bar, Progress Ring (a donut meter), and Rating (stars).

The **Arrow** tool lives here too, but arrows are deep enough to deserve their own guide: see [Arrows](/help/palette/arrows/).

A marker is a small status symbol shown inside a shape: a traffic-light dot or a checkbox. It lets you flag status at a glance, such as a red, amber, or green status, or a to-do that is done or not, without adding a separate element to the canvas.

<Figure caption="A traffic-light dot and a checkbox marker sit just left of each shape's label.">
  <ShapeMarkers />
</Figure>

## The markers

There are five markers, plus a **None** option that clears it:

- **Green circle**, **Orange circle**, and **Red circle**, filled status dots.
- **Checkbox (unchecked)**, an empty box for a "to do".
- **Checkbox (checked)**, a ticked box for "done".

## Where the marker sits

The marker sits just to the left of the shape's text. If you later clear the label, the marker stays and is centred in the shape instead. It composes neatly with an inline icon: the marker hugs the label while the icon keeps its chosen side.

You can also size the marker. The **Size** options mirror the text-size control:

- **Scale** (the default) tracks the shape's text size, so the marker grows and shrinks with the label.
- **Small**, **Medium**, and **Large** give fixed dot sizes.

## Adding a marker

Markers are managed from the single-shape right-click menu. They decorate the label, so the controls only appear once the shape has text.

<Step n={1}>Give the shape a label if it doesn't have one yet.</Step>
<Step n={2}>Right-click the shape to open its context menu.</Step>
<Step n={3}>
  Open the **Text** menu and expand the **Markers** accordion (it sits alongside Typography and
  Alignment).
</Step>
<Step n={4}>Pick one of the illustrated tiles: None, or one of the five markers.</Step>
<Step n={5}>Once a marker is chosen, a Size row appears so you can set Scale, S, M, or L.</Step>

Markers are offered for shapes only, not for arrows, text, images, or tables, and they do not appear in the multi-selection menu.

<Tip>
  Use a row of shapes with green, amber, and red dots as a quick status board, then flip a dot's
  colour from the Markers category as work progresses.
</Tip>

Auto-Attach Arrows is a toggle, and it is off by default: arrows stay pinned exactly where you drew them until you opt in. You will find it behind the gear (settings) icon in the header of the floating palette, which opens a small settings popover.

<Figure caption="The palette settings popover, with Auto-attach arrows highlighted and switched on.">
  <PaletteSettings highlight="auto-attach" />
</Figure>

## What it does

When the setting is on, arrows re-pin to the nearest face or edge of a shape as that shape moves. The connector follows along, so it keeps pointing at the right spot without you having to tidy up endpoints by hand. In the settings popover the hint reads: "Re-pin arrows to the nearest face as shapes move."

<Figure caption="As the target shape moves, the arrow re-pins to its nearest face instead of dangling at the old spot.">
  <AutoAttachArrows />
</Figure>

## When to turn it on

Switch it on if you move shapes around a lot and want connectors to keep pointing at the nearest face without tidying endpoints by hand. Leave it off (the default) when you have positioned connectors deliberately and want every endpoint to hold exactly where you put it as shapes are dragged.

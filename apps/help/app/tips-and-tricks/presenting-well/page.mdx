A finished diagram shows everything at once, which is great for reference but harder to narrate. livediagram gives you a few ways to focus an audience as you walk through your work, whether you are on a call, a projector, or sharing your screen.

## Zen mode

When you want the full canvas with nothing else competing for attention, [Zen mode](/help/tools/zen-mode/) hides every part of the interface, the header, tab bar, palette, and panels, leaving only the canvas and zoom controls. It is purely a view setting: it changes nothing about the diagram and is not shared with other people, so each viewer focuses independently. Toggle it with `Z` (or the button on the zoom controls), and exit with `Escape`.

## The laser pointer

For pointing things out live, switch to the **Laser** tool (`K`). Moving the pointer leaves a glowing trail in your participant colour that fades after about a second, and on a shared session other people see your laser in real time, so you can sweep across the diagram and direct the room's attention.

<Figure caption="The laser leaves a glowing trail in your colour that fades behind the pointer as you sweep.">
  <LaserPointer />
</Figure>

## The spotlight

To focus on one element at a time, switch to the [Spotlight](/help/palette/spotlight/) tool. It dims the rest of the canvas and lights up whatever sits under your cursor, so the room's eye lands exactly where yours does. Like the laser, it is shared live in a session, so move the cursor from shape to shape to walk through a diagram one piece at a time without anyone losing their place.
